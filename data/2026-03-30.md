### [1] [Physics-Informed Neural Network Approach for Surface Wave Propagation in Functionally Graded Magnetoelastic Layered Media](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25748)

Main category: physics.comp-ph

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一种基于物理信息神经网络（PINN）的方法，用于分析SH波在预应力和重力作用下的功能梯度磁弹性正交各向异性层状复合结构中的传播特性，并与解析解进行了验证。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究SH波在复杂层状复合结构中的传播问题，传统方法难以处理功能梯度、预应力、磁弹性和重力等多重耦合效应，需要开发高效准确的计算方法。

Method: 采用物理信息神经网络（PINN）框架，将相速度作为可训练参数，通过Adam优化器最小化损失函数，系统研究了不同激活函数和网络架构对性能的影响。

Result: PINN预测结果与解析解高度一致，误差分析表明该方法具有高精度，能够有效分析不同材料参数下相速度随波数的变化规律。

Conclusion: 提出的PINN方法能够准确分析复杂层状复合结构中的色散关系，为类似多物理场耦合问题提供了一种有效的深度学习解决方案。

Abstract: This paper investigates propagation of SH-waves in a layered composite structure consisting of a pre-stressed functionally graded magnetoelastic orthotropic layer overlying a pre-stressed functionally graded orthotropic half-space under the influence of gravity. The study introduces a physics-informed neural network (PINN) framework for the dispersion analysis of SH-waves in the considered composite medium. As a benchmark, an analytical solution to the dispersion relation is derived and used to validate accuracy and reliability of the proposed PINN formulation. In the developed PINN model, the phase velocity corresponding to a prescribed wave number is treated as a trainable parameter, enabling the determination of the dispersion relation associated with the nonlinear eigenvalue problem. The Adam optimizer is employed to minimize the loss function during the training process. Furthermore, the variation of phase velocity with wave number under different material parameters is investigated. The comparison between the analytical and PINN-based results demonstrates excellent agreement, confirming the effectiveness of the proposed deep learning approach for analysing dispersion relations in complex layered composite structures.

### [2] [Searches for Continuous Gravitational Waves from Supernova Remnants in the first part of the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA Fourth Observing run](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25808)

Main category: gr-qc

TL;DR: 对15个超新星遗迹进行连续引力波定向搜索，未发现信号，给出了最严格的应变振幅上限约4×10⁻²⁶，以及中子星椭圆度和r模式振幅的约束


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 使用LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA合作组第四次观测运行前8个月的数据，采用5种分析流程：4种半相干方法（Band-Sampled-Data、Weave和两种Viterbi）以及基于互相关的PyStoch，覆盖宽频带且不依赖目标星历先验知识

Result: 未从15个源中发现任何信号证据；在300 Hz附近对G266.2-1.2（Vela Jr.）给出了最严格的95%置信水平应变振幅上限约4×10⁻²⁶；在400 Hz以上频率对同一源给出了中子星椭圆度≲10⁻⁷和r模式振幅≲10⁻⁵的最佳约束

Abstract: We present results from directed searches for continuous gravitational waves from a sample of 15 nearby supernova remnants, likely hosting young neutron star candidates, using data from the first eight months of the fourth observing run (O4) of the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA Collaboration. These results represent the most sensitive wide-band directed searches for continuous gravitational waves from supernova remnants to date.

### [3] [Detectability and Systematic Bias from First-Order Phase-Transition Dephasing in Kerr EMRIs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25897)

Result: 获得小失配但大累积相位差（ΔΦ22^SF∼5×10^3 rad），表明窄相变窗口能产生大的相干变形，信号处于偏差敏感区域。

Abstract: We study gravitational-wave dephasing induced by an effective first-order phase transition in a Kerr extreme mass-ratio inspiral (EMRI). The transition is modeled phenomenologically as a finite-width restructuring of the dissipative flux sector, and its observational consequences are quantified with standard LISA matched-filter diagnostics. For a representative system with $M=2\times10^{5}M_\odot$, $μ=1.4M_\odot$, and $\hat a=0.90$, we obtain $ρ_{\rm B}=5.064$, $ρ_{\rm T}=4.073$, $ρ_{\rm R}=1.051$, and a mismatch $\mathcal M=2.986\times10^{-3}$ after maximization over extrinsic time and phase shifts. The resulting signal therefore lies in a bias-sensitive regime, characterized by small mismatch, order-unity residual norm, and large cumulative dephasing. Our results suggest that the dominant consequence of the transition sector is not loss of detectability, but loss of faithfulness for precision inference. This motivates future LISA EMRI waveform models that incorporate parameterized transition sectors directly into the waveform manifold.
