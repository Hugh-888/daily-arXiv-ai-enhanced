### [1] [Hybrid Classical--Quantum Optimization of Wireless Routing Using QAOA and Quantum Walks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01250)
*Eric Howard,Hardique Dasore,Hom Nath Dhungana,Radhika Kuttala,Samuel Murphy,Emma Soo,Shah Haque*

Main category: quant-ph

TL;DR: 本文探讨了混合经典-量子方法在无线路由中的应用，重点研究QAOA和量子行走如何解决无线路由中的高维约束优化问题，指出量子路由的优势在于处理困难的组合子问题而非完全替代经典框架。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 无线通信网络中的路由受移动性、干扰、拥塞和竞争服务需求的影响，使得路由选择成为高维约束优化问题而非简单的路径最短问题。传统方法难以有效处理这种复杂性，需要探索量子计算方法在路由优化中的潜力。

Method: 将无线路由问题表达为约束图优化问题，将路由目标、流量约束、连接要求和干扰效应映射到量子兼容的哈密顿表示。采用混合架构：经典系统负责网络监控、图构建、预处理和部署，量子子程序用于特定优化组件。重点研究量子近似优化算法（QAOA）和量子行走作为探索复杂路由空间的机制。

Result: 分析表明，量子路由的潜在价值主要在于处理困难的组合子问题，而不是端到端地替代经典路由框架。研究还识别了实际限制：状态准备、约束编码、oracle构建、硬件噪声、有限量子比特资源和混合执行开销。

Conclusion: 任何有意义的近期优势都取决于仔细的问题分解、紧凑编码和紧密的经典-量子集成。量子路由应被视为经典路由框架的补充而非替代，通过处理特定困难子问题来增强整体系统性能。

Abstract: Routing in wireless communication networks is shaped by mobility, interference, congestion, and competing service requirements, making route selection a high-dimensional constrained optimization problem rather than a simple shortest-path task. This paper investigates the use of hybrid classical--quantum methods for wireless routing, focusing on the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) and quantum walks as candidate mechanisms for exploring complex routing spaces. The paper examines how wireless routing can be expressed as a constrained graph optimization problem in which routing objectives, flow constraints, connectivity requirements, and interference effects are mapped into quantum-compatible Hamiltonian representations. It then discusses how these approaches can be integrated into a hybrid architecture in which classical systems perform network monitoring, graph construction, pre-processing, and deployment, while quantum subroutines are used for selected optimization components. The analysis shows that the potential value of quantum routing lies primarily in the treatment of difficult combinatorial subproblems rather than end-to-end replacement of classical routing frameworks. The paper also highlights practical limitations arising from state preparation, constraint encoding, oracle construction, hardware noise, limited qubit resources, and hybrid execution overhead. It is argued that any meaningful near-term advantage will depend on careful problem decomposition, compact encoding, and tight classical--quantum integration.

### [2] [Exhaustive Optimisation of Automorphism Groups for Stabiliser Codes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01282)
*Aisling Mac Aree,Mark Howard*

Main category: quant-ph

TL;DR: 提出一个框架，利用经典码的自同构群、逻辑基选择和码等价性，为给定量子码构造所有可实现的逻辑操作实现，并计算最优实现。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 量子码的一个重要效用指标是识别哪些逻辑操作可以在其码空间上容错实现。需要系统性地确定所有可能的逻辑操作实现，并找到最优实现，这对魔术态制备和实验应用都很重要。

Method: 引入一个框架，利用相关经典码的自同构群、逻辑基选择和码等价性，为给定[[n,k,d]]码构造所有不同的可实现的逻辑操作。建立共轭类和群横截作为关键解释概念，并计算两个可优化的性能指标。

Result: 为所有小稳定子码（n≤7，k≤2）生成了最优逻辑操作及其对应物理电路的完整表格，这些结果提供了逻辑操作的最优物理实现，对魔术态培育和实验目的都有优势。

Conclusion: 该框架能够系统性地构造和优化量子码的逻辑操作实现，为小规模稳定子码提供了完整的实现表格，这对量子计算的实际应用具有重要意义。

Abstract: An important measure of utility for a quantum code is the identification of which logical operations can be implemented fault-tolerantly on its codespace. We introduce a framework which leverages the automorphism groups of associated classical codes, the choice of logical basis and exploitation of code equivalence to construct all distinct implementable realisations of each valid logical operation for a given $[[n,k,d]]$ code. We establish conjugacy classes and group transversals (unrelated to transversality) as key explanatory concepts. We subsequently motivate and calculate two figures-of-merit that can be optimised with this framework. Our results yield a table of optimal logical operations and their corresponding physical circuits for all small stabiliser codes with $n \leq 7$ and $k \leq 2$, drawn from quantum databases. This exhaustive table of results provides the optimal physical implementations of logical operations which may be advantageous for both magic state cultivation and experimental purposes.

</details>
